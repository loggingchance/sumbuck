# SumBuck User Manual

## 1. What SumBuck Is

SumBuck is a hardwood log bucking and grading training app. It lets a user practice deciding where to buck a tree-length stem into logs, see how the resulting logs grade under the selected market rules, and compare the user’s bucking decision with the highest-value bucking pattern found by the app’s optimizer.

The app has three main work areas:

1. **Buck** — practice bucking full stems into logs.
2. **Grade** — practice grading individual pre-cut logs.
3. **Enter Specs and Prices** — review or change prices, grade names, grading standards, log rule, and custom market settings.

SumBuck is intended as a training tool. It helps users learn to see a stem in terms of log length, small-end diameter, sweep, heartwood, visible defects, face quality, grade thresholds, and market value.

## 2. Basic Screen Layout

When the app opens, the screen is divided into two main areas.

The left side is the control panel. It contains the three tabs: **Buck**, **Grade**, and **Enter Specs and Prices**. The selected tab determines what controls are shown.

The main part of the screen displays either a full stem for bucking practice, a single log for grading practice, or the results of a bucking decision.

At the top of the app, SumBuck displays the app name, the tagline, and a link to Lumbermen OS.

## 3. Bucking Practice

### 3.1 Purpose of the Buck Tab

The **Buck** tab is the main training area. In this tab, the user selects a species, market, log set, and practice stem. The user then places bucking cuts on the stem, scores the resulting logs, and compares the decision to the app’s optimal result.

The goal is not simply to make legal logs. The goal is to learn how different bucking choices affect grade recovery and total value.

### 3.2 Choosing a Market

The **Market** selector controls which price and specification set is used.

The default market is **Northern Hardwoods Market**. This uses the app’s built-in price and grade settings.

The other choice is a **Custom market** for the selected species. Custom markets are created in the **Enter Specs and Prices** tab. A custom market allows the user to change prices, grade names, and grading requirements.

Changing the market can change the best bucking pattern. A cut that is correct under one price set may not be correct under another.

### 3.3 Choosing a Species

The **Species** selector changes the species used for the exercise. The available species are:

- hard maple
- soft maple
- red oak
- black oak
- white oak
- white ash
- hickory
- yellow birch
- black cherry
- tulip poplar
- walnut

Species matters because prices and grade standards can vary by species. The app also adjusts heartwood behavior for some species, including yellow birch, black cherry, and walnut.

### 3.4 Choosing a Log Set

The **Log set** selector has two choices:

- **Practice logs**
- **Instruction logs**

Practice logs are the larger set of generated training stems. They are useful for repetition and skill-building.

Instruction logs are a smaller set of teaching stems. When an instruction log is selected, the left panel shows an **Instruction points** section. This section explains the main lesson of the stem and may include tags such as veneer, sweep, defect placement, trim, diameter thresholds, or market comparison.

Instruction logs should generally be used first when learning. Practice logs are better for repeated independent practice.

### 3.5 Choosing a Stem

The **Practice stem** or **Instruction stem** selector chooses the specific stem. The **Next** button advances to the next stem in the current log set.

When a new stem is selected, existing cuts are cleared and the exercise starts fresh.

### 3.6 Reading the Stem Display

The stem display shows the selected stem as a log graphic. It includes:

- the stem length in feet
- the species
- the active log rule
- visible defects
- face views
- length marks along the stem
- cut lines when cuts have been placed

The stem is shown as a training model, not as a photograph. Defects are simplified visual marks intended to help the user practice bucking and grading decisions.

### 3.7 Rolling the Stem

The stem has four faces: **face 1**, **face 2**, **face 3**, and **face 4**.

Use **Roll left**, **Roll right**, the roll slider, or the face buttons to inspect different faces. The label near the roll controls shows which face is currently up.

Rolling the stem is important because a defect may appear on one face and not another. The value of a log often depends on whether defects fall on the faces that control grade.

### 3.8 Using the Magnifier

The **Magnifier** button turns the inspection magnifier on or off. When the magnifier is on, moving over the stem gives a closer look at the stem surface and marks.

Use the magnifier when defect placement is close to a proposed cut, when a defect is subtle, or when comparing faces.

### 3.9 Placing Cuts Manually

To place a cut manually, click on the stem at the desired position. A cut line appears at that point.

A cut divides the stem into segments. Each segment is later evaluated as a potential log. Cuts can be moved by dragging the cut handle, unless the result has already been submitted.

Use manual cuts when a defect location or diameter threshold requires a precise cut that is not simply the next standard length.

### 3.10 Using Quick Cuts

The **Quick cuts** buttons add standard nominal log lengths from the current opening. The available quick cuts are:

- 8 feet
- 9 feet
- 10 feet
- 11 feet
- 12 feet
- 13 feet
- 14 feet
- 15 feet
- 16 feet

A quick cut includes the app’s trim allowance. For example, an 8-foot quick cut places the cut at the length needed to make an 8-foot nominal log with trim.

Use quick cuts when building a normal bucking pattern from the butt toward the top.

### 3.11 Backing Up

The **Back** button removes the most recent cut. This is useful when testing a possible pattern and then reconsidering the last decision.

### 3.12 Clearing Cuts

The **Clear cuts** button removes all cuts from the stem and returns the exercise to an unsubmitted state.

Use this when starting over on the same stem.

### 3.13 Scoring a Bucking Pattern

After placing cuts, click **Score bucker**.

The app then evaluates the user’s logs and displays the bucker value. It also shows summary boxes and a results table. The results include information such as:

- log number
- length
- small-end diameter
- heartwood diameter and heartwood percent
- sweep
- grade
- volume
- price per MBF
- value
- grading notes

Scoring is useful when the user wants to evaluate the chosen bucking pattern without immediately seeing the optimal answer.

### 3.14 Editing After Scoring

After scoring, click **Edit cuts** to return to the cut layout and revise the decision.

This allows the user to learn by trial, correction, and re-scoring.

### 3.15 Comparing With the Optimal Result

Click **Compare optimal** to see the user’s bucking pattern beside the app’s highest-value pattern.

The comparison view shows:

- the bucker’s pattern and total value
- the optimal pattern and total value
- the dollar difference
- comparison tables for the user and optimal logs
- feedback notes explaining where value may have been lost

The optimal result is the best result found by the app under the current settings. It depends on the selected species, prices, grade standards, trim rules, defects, sweep deductions, and selected log rule.

The optimal result should be treated as a training target. It is not a field rule that must always be followed without judgment. Real-world bucking may also consider mill instructions, handling limits, visible and hidden defects, risk, order requirements, and buyer preferences.

## 4. Grading Practice

### 4.1 Purpose of the Grade Tab

The **Grade** tab trains the user to identify the grade of pre-cut logs. Instead of bucking a full stem, the user is shown one log at a time and asked to choose its grade.

This is useful for learning the relationship between diameter, length, heartwood, sweep, clear faces or clear cuttings, and defects.

### 4.2 Choosing Species for Grading

Use the **Species** selector to choose the species for the grading exercises. Changing species resets the grading exercise to the first log for that species.

### 4.3 Keeping Score

The **keep score** checkbox controls whether the app tracks grading performance.

When scorekeeping is on, the app shows:

- overall score
- number correct out of number answered
- score for the last 10 answers
- whether the current answer is right, wrong, or still open

Use **Reset grading score** to clear the grading score and begin again.

### 4.4 Inspecting a Grading Log

The grading screen shows one pre-cut log. Use **Roll left**, **Roll right**, the roll slider, the face buttons, and **Magnifier** to inspect the log.

The grading panel displays key measurements:

- diameter
- length
- heartwood percent
- sweep
- clear faces or clear cuttings, depending on the active grading method

These measurements are the main clues for selecting the correct grade.

### 4.5 Selecting a Grade

The grade choices are shown as buttons. The default grade names are:

- slicer veneer
- rotary veneer
- prime sawlog
- select sawlog
- #1 sawlog
- #2 sawlog
- #3 sawlog
- scrag

Click the grade that best fits the log, then click **Submit grade**.

After submission, the app shows whether the answer is correct and gives an explanation. The explanation identifies the correct grade and summarizes the controlling measurements. It also shows notes about eligibility for the grade.

### 4.6 Moving to the Next Grading Log

Click **Next log** to advance to the next grading exercise.

For training, it is usually best to study the explanation before moving on.

## 5. Entering Specifications and Prices

### 5.1 Purpose of the Specs and Prices Tab

The **Enter Specs and Prices** tab controls the market assumptions used by both bucking and grading.

This tab allows the user to:

- switch between the Northern Hardwoods Market and a custom market
- choose the log rule
- edit prices by species and grade
- change grade names
- change grade standards
- save, load, or clear one custom market

Changes made here can affect bucking results, grading results, and the optimal comparison.

### 5.2 Market Setup

The **Market setup** section has two choices:

- **Northern Hardwoods Market**
- **Custom species market**

The Northern Hardwoods Market is the built-in default.

The custom species market is the editable market for the currently selected species.

### 5.3 Saving a Custom Market

When using a custom market, enter a **Custom market name** and click **Save custom market**.

The free version saves one custom species market at a time. Saving a new custom market replaces the previously saved custom market.

Use **Load saved** to restore the saved custom market. Use **Clear saved** to remove it.

Custom market information is stored in the browser on the current computer. It is not a shared account setting. A different browser or device will not automatically have the same custom market.

### 5.4 Choosing the Log Rule

The **Log rule** selector controls the board-foot calculation used for value estimates. The available rules are:

- International 1/4
- Doyle
- Scribner

Changing the log rule changes calculated volume and value. It may also change which bucking pattern produces the best value.

### 5.5 Editing Prices

The **Prices** section shows prices for the selected species in dollars per MBF.

Each grade has its own price field. Prices can be changed in $25 increments, although any nonnegative number can be entered.

The grade price affects the optimizer. If veneer is priced much higher than sawlogs, the optimizer will protect veneer more aggressively. If lower-grade logs have stronger prices, the optimizer may accept simpler bucking patterns that produce more volume with less grade risk.

### 5.6 Editing Grade Names

Each grade card has a **grade name** field. This allows the user to rename the displayed grade for a custom market.

For example, a user could rename **scrag** to match a local buyer term.

Changing a grade name changes display labels. It does not by itself change the grading rule. To change the grading rule, edit the measurement and defect fields in the grade card.

### 5.7 Sawlog Grading Method

The **Sawlog grading method for this price set** controls how sawlogs are judged. The available methods are:

- **clear faces**
- **clear cuttings**

One price set uses one sawlog grading method throughout. Changing this setting applies the same method to every sawlog grade below.

Use **clear faces** when the rule is based mainly on the number of clear faces.

Use **clear cuttings** when the rule is based on defect-free cuttings on the grading faces.

### 5.8 Grade Standard Fields

Each grade card includes fields that define whether a log qualifies for that grade.

The main fields are:

- **min SED** — minimum small-end diameter in inches.
- **lengths** — allowed nominal lengths for the grade.
- **clear faces** — minimum number of clear faces when using the clear-face method.
- **min cutting ft** — minimum clear cutting length when using the clear-cutting method.
- **max cuttings** — maximum number of clear cuttings allowed.
- **clear portion** — required share of the grading face that must be clear.
- **max heart %** — maximum heartwood percent allowed, if any.
- **max sweep** — maximum sweep allowed, if any.
- **ignore defects 2\" or less fully inside 6\" trim** — allows small trim-zone defects to be ignored for sawlog grading when applicable.
- **notes** — text notes describing the grade rule.

If a field is left blank where the app allows blanks, it generally means that no limit is being applied for that field.

### 5.9 Restoring Defaults

Click **Use Northern Hardwoods defaults** to restore the built-in grade standards for the selected species.

This is useful after experimenting with custom specs.

## 6. Understanding the Results

### 6.1 Log Length and Trim

SumBuck uses nominal log lengths and trim. In bucking practice, quick cuts add six inches of trim to the nominal log length. A cut for an 8-foot log therefore uses an 8-foot log plus trim.

The app also has a one-inch sawlog trim allowance in some calculations. This may allow a sawlog to remain legal when the difference is small. Veneer should not be forced into short-trim situations.

### 6.2 Small-End Diameter

Small-end diameter is measured at the top end of each log segment. Many grades have minimum small-end diameter requirements. As the stem tapers, an upper log may lose eligibility for a higher grade even if its faces are otherwise good.

### 6.3 Heartwood

Some products and species are sensitive to heartwood percentage. The app reports heartwood diameter and heartwood percentage for each segment.

High heart percentage may block a grade if the selected standard has a maximum heart limit.

### 6.4 Sweep

Sweep is reported in inches. If a grade has a maximum sweep limit, too much sweep can downgrade the log.

Sweep can make a long high-grade-looking log less valuable than two shorter logs or a lower-grade product.

### 6.5 Defects

The app distinguishes among different defect effects:

- defects that block veneer only
- defects that affect sawlog grade
- defects that affect both veneer and sawlog grade
- informational marks

This distinction is important. A veneer-only mark may prevent veneer but may not reduce sawlog grade. A sawlog-affecting defect may reduce clear faces or clear cuttings and push the log into a lower grade.

### 6.6 Grade Eligibility Notes

When a log is scored or graded, the app provides eligibility notes. These notes explain why a log did or did not qualify for a product.

Use these notes as the main learning tool. They show whether the controlling issue was diameter, length, heart, sweep, defects, or face quality.

### 6.7 Dollar Value

The app calculates value as volume multiplied by price per MBF. The selected log rule affects volume. The selected price table affects value.

The best bucking pattern is not always the one with the highest grade on the butt log. The best pattern is the one that creates the best total value from the whole stem under the current market assumptions.

## 7. Suggested Training Routine

A new user should start with this sequence:

1. Open the **Buck** tab.
2. Select **Instruction logs**.
3. Read the instruction point before cutting.
4. Roll the stem and inspect all faces.
5. Place a bucking pattern.
6. Click **Score bucker**.
7. Study the result table and grade notes.
8. Click **Compare optimal**.
9. Study the dollar difference and feedback notes.
10. Click **Next** and repeat.

After completing several instruction logs, move to **Practice logs** and try to make decisions before comparing with the optimizer.

For grading practice, use this sequence:

1. Open the **Grade** tab.
2. Select a species.
3. Inspect the log on all four faces.
4. Read the diameter, length, heart, sweep, and clear-face or clear-cutting information.
5. Select a grade.
6. Submit the grade.
7. Read the explanation before moving to the next log.

## 8. Common Mistakes to Watch For

### 8.1 Cutting Before Looking at All Faces

A defect on the far side of the stem may control grade. Roll the stem before deciding.

### 8.2 Chasing Veneer Too Far Up the Stem

Veneer is often a lower-stem opportunity. Upper-stem diameter, sweep, heart, and defects can make veneer unrealistic.

### 8.3 Ignoring Small-End Diameter

A log can look clear but fail a grade because the small end is too small.

### 8.4 Overreacting to Veneer-Only Defects

A veneer-only defect may block veneer but may not hurt sawlog grade. Do not automatically sacrifice sawlog value because of a defect that only affects veneer.

### 8.5 Forgetting Trim

Quick cuts include trim. Manual cuts must still create legal nominal log lengths. A visually close cut may be short after trim is considered.

### 8.6 Assuming the Highest Individual Log Grade Creates the Highest Stem Value

The best stem value depends on the total set of logs, not just one attractive butt log.

### 8.7 Comparing Results Under Different Markets

If the market, log rule, species, prices, or specifications change, the optimal result may change. Compare bucking patterns only under the same settings.

## 9. Troubleshooting

### 9.1 The App Still Shows an Old Version

Refresh the browser with a hard refresh. On Windows, use **Ctrl + Shift + R** or **Ctrl + F5**. Opening the app in a private or incognito window can also bypass cached files.

### 9.2 Custom Prices or Specs Disappeared

Custom settings are stored in the browser. They may disappear if browser storage is cleared or if the app is opened in a different browser or device.

### 9.3 A Saved Custom Market Is Not the One Expected

The free version saves one custom species market at a time. Saving a new custom market replaces the old saved custom market.

### 9.4 The Optimal Result Seems Surprising

Check the selected species, market, log rule, prices, grade standards, trim rules, and defects. The optimizer is using the current settings. A surprising result often comes from a changed price, a diameter threshold, sweep, or a defect that controls grade.

### 9.5 A Quick Cut Button Is Disabled

A quick cut button may be disabled if there is not enough remaining stem length to make that nominal log length with trim.

## 10. Glossary

**Bucking** — cutting a tree-length stem into logs.

**Clear cutting** — a defect-free length on a grading face, used by some factory-log style grading rules.

**Clear face** — a face of the log that is free enough of grade-affecting defects to count toward a grade.

**Custom market** — user-edited price and specification settings for a selected species.

**Face** — one of four sides of the log used for visual inspection and grading.

**Heartwood percent** — heartwood diameter as a percentage of total diameter.

**Log rule** — the board-foot volume rule used to calculate volume, such as International 1/4, Doyle, or Scribner.

**MBF** — one thousand board feet.

**Nominal length** — the named log length, such as 8 feet, 10 feet, or 16 feet, not including trim.

**Optimizer** — the app calculation that searches for the highest-value legal bucking pattern under the current settings.

**Scrag** — the lowest product class in the app’s default product list.

**SED** — small-end diameter, measured at the top end of the log.

**Sweep** — curvature in the log that can reduce grade or value.

**Trim** — extra length beyond the nominal log length.

**Veneer-only defect** — a defect that blocks veneer recovery but does not necessarily reduce sawlog grade.

## 11. Limits of the App

SumBuck is a training model. It does not replace buyer specifications, mill instructions, field judgment, or professional log grading. Actual log value can also be affected by hidden defects, mill equipment, order files, species-specific buyer preferences, trucking and handling limits, and local market conditions.

Use SumBuck to build judgment, compare alternatives, and learn the value consequences of bucking and grading decisions.
